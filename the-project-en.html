Strategic Objectives

The academic framework uniting scientific rigour and intercultural dialogue to tackle global sustainability challenges.

Context and Motivation

The complexity of contemporary challenges, as outlined by the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), demands a reshaping of how new scientists are trained. Problems such as food security and soil contamination do not respect geopolitical borders.

The Crossing Boundaries project was conceived to bridge the gap between traditional technical training and the global competencies required in the 21st century. By connecting Durham University and the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (UFRJ), we have established a transnational laboratory for innovation.

Our premise is that cultural diversity is not an obstacle, but a catalyst for solving complex problems in chemistry and biotechnology.

Scientific Innovation

Developing tangible prototypes of sustainable solutions, such as biodegradable nanopesticides and bioinoculants adapted to tropical soils.

Internationalisation

Fostering intercultural competence, preparing students to operate within high-level multidisciplinary and multilingual teams.

Social Impact

Translating complex science into direct social benefits, aligned with the Zero Hunger and Life on Land goals of the 2030 Agenda.

The Pedagogical Approach

What is COIL?

Collaborative Online International Learning is a methodology that connects classrooms in different countries, creating a shared environment where knowledge is co-constructed through cultural diversity.

Weeks 1-2

Connection and Planning

The initial phase focuses on ice-breaking. Students from Brazil and the UK are introduced via Zoom, form mixed groups, and define their communication channels.

Weeks 3-8

Collaborative Research

The core of scientific production. While UFRJ students collect data on tropical soils, peers in Durham simulate conditions in the laboratory. Asynchronous data exchange occurs continuously.

Weeks 9-10

Presentation & Reflection

Final presentation of projects to an international panel. Consolidation of data and feedback on the acquired intercultural competence.

Methodological Manifesto

The Essence of COIL

Collaborative Online International Learning (COIL) is not just technology, but a teaching philosophy that democratises international experience. It allows students who might never travel physically to have a deep cultural immersion.

How does it work in practice?

Unlike traditional online courses (MOOCs), COIL relies on synchronous and asynchronous interaction. Professors from Durham and UFRJ design a joint curriculum where tasks can only be solved through collaboration between peers.

  • Negotiation of Meaning: Students must explain technical concepts in a second language.
  • Conflict Management: Time zone differences and working styles become part of the learning process.
  • Co-creation: The final product (report/prototype) is of shared authorship.

In the Crossing Boundaries project, COIL is the vehicle that allows cutting-edge science to dialogue with the local needs of Brazilian soil.
