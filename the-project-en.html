About the Initiative

Connecting Knowledge,
Transcending Boundaries.

How international collaboration between Brazil and the UK is redefining science education for sustainable development.

The Global Challenge

The Crossing Boundaries project stems from an urgent need: to prepare scientists to work in a globalized world, where complex problems — such as those defined by the UN SDGs — require more than just technical knowledge.

We investigate how students of Chemistry (Durham) and Microbiology (UFRJ) can collaborate remotely, overcoming linguistic and cultural barriers to create tangible scientific solutions.

Key Objectives

  • Investigate the impact of intercultural dialogue on solving scientific problems.
  • Develop a replicable COIL (Collaborative Online International Learning) pedagogy.
  • Propose real solutions for the SDGs (Zero Hunger, Life on Land, Clean Water).
Methodology

The 10-Week Journey

A structured process of remote collaboration, student autonomy, and hybrid supervision.

Week 1-2: Kick-off & Formation

Random division into mixed groups (UK/BR). Autonomous definition of working platforms and schedules.

Week 3-8: Development & Supervision

Bi-weekly hybrid meetings with supervisors. Completion of reflective diaries on the intercultural experience.

Preparation of the final project, simulating a proposal for real funding agencies.

Week 10: Showcase

Presentation of solutions to the "Chemistry and Society" class at Durham University.

Science Meets Culture

The project's strength lies not only in chemistry but in the fusion of fields. Intercultural researchers monitor every step, analyzing how language and culture shape science.
