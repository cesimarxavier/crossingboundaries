Biodegradable Nanopesticides: Sustainable Soil Protection

A silica-based encapsulation system that reduces the leaching of toxic chemicals by 40%, protecting the water table in areas of intensive agriculture.

The Challenge

The indiscriminate use of pesticides in tropical agriculture faces a dual problem: rapid degradation of active ingredients by intense UV radiation and leaching into groundwater due to torrential rains.

This forces farmers to apply higher doses than necessary, increasing costs and environmental impact, directly countering SDG 15 (Life on Land).

The Proposed Solution

The team developed a controlled delivery system using mesoporous silica nanoparticles. The innovation lies in the modification of the silica surface with natural polymers derived from biomass waste (rice husk).

  • UV Protection: The capsule shields the active ingredient from direct sunlight.
  • Slow Release: The pesticide is released gradually only in the presence of specific humidity.
  • Biodegradability: The capsule decomposes into inert silicon after use.

Preliminary Results

In bench tests conducted simultaneously in Durham and Rio de Janeiro, a 40% reduction in contamination of simulated runoff water was observed. Furthermore, efficacy against target pests remained stable for 15 days longer than the standard commercial formulation.
