Durham Delegation Strengthens Ties with UFRJ in Rio de Janeiro

Author: Prof. Patrick Steel | 15 Jan 2026 | Category: Fieldwork & Partnership

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil â€“ Last week marked a significant milestone in the Crossing Boundaries project as a delegation from Durham University arrived at the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (UFRJ) for a series of strategic meetings and laboratory visits.

The primary objective of this institutional visit was to align the pedagogical goals for the upcoming semester and to witness firsthand the infrastructure available for the Brazilian cohort of students.

Bridging the Distance

While online collaboration tools like Zoom and Slack have been instrumental in launching this project, physical presence remains a powerful catalyst for trust and deeper understanding. As Prof. Andrew Macrae noted during the opening plenary:

"Digital tools build bridges, but human connection builds the foundations. Seeing the laboratories where our students will be testing the bioinoculants allows us to better tailor the experimental protocols developed in Durham."

Key Outcomes of the Visit

During the three-day agenda, the team focused on several key areas:

  • Lab Safety Harmonisation: Establishing a unified safety protocol that respects both UK and Brazilian regulations.
  • Curriculum Design: Finalising the "Intercultural COIL Module" that will be taught jointly next term.
  • Field Site Survey: Visiting the Atlantic Forest reserve where soil samples will be collected.

Next Steps

The delegation returns to the UK with a signed Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) for the next phase of the project, which includes a reciprocal visit of UFRJ scholars to Durham later this year.

We invite the academic community to follow the progress of the pilot experiments which are set to begin next month.
